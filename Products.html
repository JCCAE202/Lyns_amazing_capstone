Disaster Response for First Responders (JC1003):

This course is a comprehensive study of the role of the disaster response counselor. After successfully completing the course, students will be able to: -- understand and discuss the spiritual responsibilities of counselors during a disaster; -- understand what type and level of response is necessary; -- understand and explain the phases of a disaster; -- understand and explain victim behaviors; and -- understand and demonstrate self-care for the on-scene counselor

Volunteer Liability (JC2004):

This course is a study of the legal issues surrounding the crisis counselor or volunteer who assists or works with individuals or communities who have suffered a traumatic event. After successfully completing the course, students will be able to understand the importance of volunteer liability; understand and discuss liability issues in crisis counseling; and understand the benefits of a comprehensive liability program and policy.

Traumatic Stress Intervention and Crisis Counseling (JC1005):

This course is an in-depth study of traumatic crisis situations. The target audiences are LPC crisis counselors and volunteers who work with individuals or communities who have suffered a traumatic event. After successfully completing the course, students will be able to: -- identify particular behaviors and actions associated with those in a crisis; -- explain stress reactions and what happens when unmanaged stress results in Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD); and -- understand and apply counseling techniques with critical incident management procedures. .

Police Suicide Awareness (JC1009):

This course is designed for crisis counselors and volunteers who work with police departments, families and survivors of police officer suicide. After successfully completing the course, students will be able to; -- explain suicide as it relates to traumatic stress; -- apply suicide training to law enforcement settings; -- recognize signs and symptoms of suicidal behavior; -- recognize triggers of trauma and suicide; and -- apply counseling techniques to suicidal subjects. .

Death Notification (JC1010):

This course is a study of the procedures faith-based counselors will use when assisting in death notifications to family, friends and co-workers. It provides information on pre-notification preparations, general guidelines, techniques in dealing with survivors and what to expect as a counselor when assisting with a death notification. .

EMS Chaplaincy (JC3131):

This course is an in-depth study of EMS Chaplaincy and the roles and responsibility of the EMS Chaplain who responds to crisis situations. The first part of the manual is for the department and will explain how to set up a chaplain program in the department. Students will learn communication and listening techniques, post-traumatic stress disorders signs and symptoms, EMS personnel suicides, line of duty deaths, death notifications, and EMS families in domestic violence, call procedures, the thin red line and public prayer. The course manual includes forms, code of ethics, instructions and general orders.
